# A Candidate Geometric Framework for Cosmological Tensions: The E2 Manifold

**Author:** Melissa Dawn Dembeck-Ellison
**Status:** Theoretical Prototype / Draft

## Abstract
We propose a candidate geometric framework that may reduce the Hubble ($H_0$) and Growth ($S_8$) tensions under simplified prototype tests. By modeling the universe as a finite Half-Turn ($E_2$) Manifold with a fundamental domain size of $L \approx 18.7$ Gpc, we investigate a leading-order correction to the volume expansion rate, manifesting as a Logarithmic Expansion Drift $H(z) = H_{late} - k \log_{10}(1+z)$.

## 1. Prototype Findings
Under our initial, heavily binned validation tests using simplified data subsets, this candidate model demonstrates a potential $\Delta AIC = 7.20$ preference over standard $\Lambda$CDM, reconciling a local $H_0 = 72.0$ km/s/Mpc while theoretically maintaining the stability of the higher CMB acoustic peaks. Furthermore, the framework predicts a natural $\sim 30\%$ suppression of structure growth history, offering a geometric pathway to resolving the $S_8$ tension.

## 2. Limitations and Required Formalization
This manuscript currently represents a theoretical proof-of-concept. The findings presented rely on representative mock data for MCMC parameter visualization and highly binned subsets of the Pantheon+ dataset. To elevate this candidate model to a confirmed resolution, subsequent work will require full integration of unbinned covariance matrices, formal Bayesian evidence generation via a completed `Cobaya`/`CAMB` likelihood integration, and full-sky polarization simulations.
