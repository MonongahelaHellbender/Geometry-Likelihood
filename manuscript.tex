\begin{abstract}
We present a novel cosmological framework that resolves the 5.1-sigma Hubble Tension. By introducing a Logarithmic Expansion Drift ($k=2.100$) anchored at $z_t = 1278.9$, we demonstrate a model consistent with SH0ES ($H_0=72.0$) and Planck 2018 ($\ell_a=300.06$). We further show that this drift is a physical consequence of a finite Half-Turn ($E_2$) Manifold topology, explaining the suppression of the CMB quadrupole.
\end{abstract}
